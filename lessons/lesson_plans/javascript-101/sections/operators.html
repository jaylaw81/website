Operators - Conditional and Logical

Comparison and Logical Operators

Comparison Operators
Comparison operators use logical statements to determine differences between variables.

Using comparison operators we can determine the values of variables. Are the variables equal? Is one variable greater than or lesser than another. Once we've determined the value of a variable or its contents we can then take action based on conditions.

Logical Operators
Logical operators are used to evaluate multiple conditions within a conditional statement.

Lets take a look at some examples of conditional and logical operators and statements.

JS Variable Operators - Less Than & Greater Than

Less Than: Left value is less than the right value.
5 < 6 : Results TRUE

Greater Than: Left value is greater than the right value.
6 > 5 : Results TRUE

JS Variable Operators - Less Than or Equal To & Greater Than or Equal To

Less Than or Equal to: Left value is less than or equal to the right value.
4 <= 6 : Results TRUE
6 <= 6 : Results TRUE

Greater Than or Equal to: Left value is greater than or equal to the right value.
8 >= 6 : Results TRUE
6 >= 6 : Results TRUE

JS Variable Operators - Equal To & NOT Equal To

Equal To: Left value is equal to the right value.
"6" == 6 : Results TRUE
6 == 6 : Results TRUE

NOT Equal To: Left value is not equal to the right value.
6 != 5 : Results TRUE
6 != 6 : Results FALSE

JS Variable Operators STRICT - Equal To & NOT Equal To

Equal To: Left value is equal to the right value.
6 === 6 : Results TRUE
"6" === 6 : Results FALSE

NOT Equal To: Left value is not equal to the right value.
"6" !=== 6 : Results TRUE
6 !=== 6 : Results FALSE

JS Variable Operator - OR "||"

OR (||) - Operator for checking if ANY conditions are true.
5 < 6 || 6 < 5 : Finds at least one TRUE condition - Results TRUE.

JS Variable Operator - AND "&&"

AND (&&) - Operator for checking if ALL conditions are true.
5 < 6 && 6 < 5 : Finds at least one FALSE condition - Results FALSE.

JS Variable Operator - NOT "!"

NOT (!) - Operator for checking the opposite a conditional result or to check if a parameter is NOT a certain value.
!false : Results TRUE

JS Variable Operator - Modulus "%"

Modulus - This is the REMAINDER of the left value divided by the right value.
Condition : 12 % 3 == 0 : Results TRUE
Examples:
3 % 3 = 0
10 % 3 = 1
12 % 3 = 0
14 % 3 = 2
